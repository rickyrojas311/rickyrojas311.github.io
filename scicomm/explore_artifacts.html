Artifact Focus Affects Form

Chronological Timeline

When comparing the form and focus of artifacts, we can see a positive correlation between how visual it is an its focus on human learning. Artifacts that are focused on human learning tend to take more illustrative and diverse froms whereas artifacts with a computational focus tend to be more textual or otherwise more informationally dense.

General Audience

Communicating with a general audience requires transforming complex data into accessible, engaging narratives. This often involves using highly visual formats like infographics and physical exhibits. The goal is to capture attention and convey key messages without requiring prior expertise. Artifacts for this audience prioritize intuitive design, storytelling, and tactile interaction to make abstract concepts tangible and memorable. The variety in form, from animated videos to interactive museum pieces, reflects the need to meet the audience where they are and spark curiosity.

Specialized Audience

When communicating with a specialized audience, the focus shifts from broad engagement to technical precision and depth. These artifacts, typically research papers and conference presentations, assume a shared foundation of knowledge. The priority is to clearly present methodology, data, and findings to facilitate expert scrutiny and contribute to the academic discourse. While clarity is still crucial, visualizations are often more data-dense, and the language is more technical, aiming for replicability and a detailed, nuanced discussion among peers.
