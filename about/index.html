About

I'm an undergraduate and master's student at Stanford, finishing a B.S. with Honors in Data Science — with a subplan in Mathematical and Computational Science and an Honors Concentration in Computational Neuroscience — alongside a concurrent M.S. in Statistics. I also completed the Notation in Science Communication with Distinction, which is where the SciComm portfolio comes from.

My research sits in speech neuroscience: I'm currently a research assistant in Prof. Laura Gwilliams's lab, studying how the brain resolves syntax during natural speech comprehension. Earlier work took me through Prof. Russell Poldrack's lab and the Radiological Sciences Laboratory, where I worked on MRI reconstruction methods. Across all of it, I keep coming back to the same tools: neural decoding models, statistical inference, and — more recently — fine-tuning language models with reinforcement learning.

I also teach: I'm a teaching assistant for Stanford's introductory data science course and a peer advisor for the Data Science department. For the fuller history — degrees, positions, publications, grants — see the CV. To get in touch, use the links in the footer below.
